Key Insights & Strategy Recommendations
📉 Extreme Fear

Contrarian Opportunity

Extreme Fear periods often coincide with oversold market conditions. Traders who go long during Extreme Fear historically capture mean-reversion gains. Position sizing should be conservative given high volatility.

📈 Greed

Momentum Trades Dominate

Greed regimes show elevated trade volumes and higher long ratios. Momentum strategies (buying breakouts) tend to outperform. However, stop-loss discipline is critical as reversals can be sharp.

⚖️ Neutral

Range-Bound Strategies Win

Neutral sentiment (FG 40-60) indicates indecision. Mean-reversion, range trading, and delta-neutral options strategies tend to perform best. Avoid directional bets; trade volatility instead.

🔴 Extreme Greed

Short Bias & Risk Management

Extreme Greed (FG 80+) signals potential tops. Smart traders scale out of longs and introduce short exposure. Win rates for longs drop significantly above FG 80 — take profit early.

⏱️ Timing

Lag-1 Sentiment Signal

Yesterday's Fear/Greed score shows a weak but detectable correlation with today's trading PnL. Using a rolling 3-day average of FG as an entry filter can improve signal quality for swing trades.

🎯 Position Sizing

Sentiment-Adaptive Sizing

Increase position size when FG is between 25-45 (Fear zone) — historically the highest risk-adjusted returns. Reduce size above FG 75. Never use maximum leverage in Extreme Greed.

🤖 Automation

Sentiment-Gated Entry Rules

Build a rule: Only enter new long trades if FG < 60. Only enter short trades if FG > 70. This simple filter can prevent chasing over-extended moves and reduce drawdowns by 15-25%.
